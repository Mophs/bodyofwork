nd we came to a town called Bounty. A border town, a wound in the sacred wilderness. The kind of place where folks begged the wild soil for grain and mud-sifters came to drink away their meagre finds. A place where the only overstayers were those too broken to crawl out. They called it Bounty ‘cos its where the blood of the unknown pooled, where what passed for civilisation trepanned at the sides of gods clammy head.

Threshold towns where where you faced god. The idea of god. You either turned your back in denial and reinvented yourself a pilgrim. Hiding in piety you went home. Some of us had our reasons, we closed our eyes in deference. We stepped under the yoke. Submitted to grief, fear, greed or to the clouded mind of youth. We were men made homunculi, a shattering of perspective and focus. Like finding your home lifted away from you and your mansion a thimble to giants.

We smelled magic in the hills and in the bones. The funeral pyre of the divine, a sooty treasure. We left a town called Bounty.
